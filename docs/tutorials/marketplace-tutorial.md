## 1. Understanding the Marketplace

### What is the Secondary Marketplace?

The marketplace allows ticket holders to resell their NFT tickets to other users before the event. It's a secure, decentralized platform that:

- **Prevents Fraud**: All tickets are verified on blockchain
- **Controls Scalping**: Price limits set by event organizers
- **Ensures Authenticity**: Impossible to create fake tickets
- **Provides Transparency**: All transactions are public
- **Protects Buyers**: Automatic escrow and transfer

### Key Features

**Anti-Scalping Protection**
- Maximum resale prices set by organizers
- Typically 20-50% above original price
- Prevents excessive markup
- Protects fans from price gouging

**Royalty System**
- Organizers earn percentage on resales
- Usually 2-10% of sale price
- Supports artists and event creators
- Encourages quality events

**Platform Fees**
- 2.5% fee on all marketplace sales
- Covers platform maintenance and development
- Much lower than traditional ticketing platforms
- Transparent and predictable

### Marketplace Sections

**Browse Tickets**
- All available tickets for sale
- Filter by event, date, price
- Sort by price, time listed
- View seller information

**My Listings**
- Tickets you've listed for sale
- Manage prices and availability
- Track views and interest
- Cancel listings if needed

**Purchase History**
- Tickets you've bought from marketplace
- Transaction details and receipts
- Transfer confirmations
- Seller information

**Sales History**
- Tickets you've sold
- Revenue and fee breakdown
- Buyer information
- Performance analytics

## 2. Buying from the Marketplace

### Step 1: Browse Available Tickets

**Access the Marketplace**
1. Go to "Marketplace" section
2. Browse available tickets
3. Use filters to narrow search:
   - Event name
   - Date range
   - Price range
   - Venue location
   - Seat section

**Ticket Information Displayed**
- Event details (name, date, venue)
- Seat number and section
- Current asking price
- Original purchase price
- Time listed
- Seller's wallet address
- Number of tickets sold by seller

### Step 2: Evaluate Listings

**Price Comparison**
```
Original Price: 0.1 ETH
Current Listings:
- Listing A: 0.12 ETH (+20%)
- Listing B: 0.115 ETH (+15%)
- Listing C: 0.13 ETH (+30%)
```

**Factors to Consider**
- **Price vs. Original**: How much markup?
- **Seat Quality**: Better seats worth premium?
- **Seller History**: How many successful sales?
- **Time Sensitivity**: Event date approaching?
- **Market Activity**: Many listings or few?

### Step 3: Make a Purchase

**Purchase Process**
1. Click on desired ticket listing
2. Review all details carefully:
   - Event information
   - Seat details
   - Total price including fees
   - Seller information
3. Click "Buy Ticket"
4. Confirm transaction in wallet
5. Wait for blockchain confirmation

**Transaction Breakdown**
```
Ticket Price: 0.12 ETH
Platform Fee (2.5%): 0.003 ETH
Organizer Royalty (5%): 0.006 ETH
Gas Fee: ~0.001 ETH
Total Cost: ~0.13 ETH

Seller Receives: 0.111 ETH
```

### Step 4: Receive Your Ticket

**After Purchase**
- Ticket NFT transfers to your wallet
- QR code becomes available
- Original listing is removed
- You can now use ticket for event entry

**Verification Steps**
1. Check ticket appears in your wallet
2. Verify event details are correct
3. Test QR code visibility
4. Save backup copies

## 3. Selling Your Tickets

### Step 1: Prepare to List

**Check Eligibility**
- Event must allow transfers
- Ticket must not be used
- Must be within transfer window
- You must own the ticket

**Gather Information**
- Original purchase price
- Current market prices
- Event date and urgency
- Seat quality and location

### Step 2: Set Your Price

**Pricing Considerations**
- **Maximum Allowed**: Set by event organizer
- **Market Rate**: What others are charging
- **Urgency**: How quickly you need to sell
- **Seat Quality**: Premium locations worth more

**Pricing Examples**
```
Original Price: 0.1 ETH
Maximum Allowed: 0.15 ETH (+50%)

Conservative Pricing: 0.11 ETH (+10%)
Market Rate: 0.125 ETH (+25%)
Premium Pricing: 0.14 ETH (+40%)
```

### Step 3: Create Your Listing

**Listing Process**
1. Go to "My Tickets"
2. Select ticket to sell
3. Click "List for Sale"
4. Set your asking price
5. Review listing details
6. Approve marketplace access (first time only)
7. Confirm listing transaction
8. Pay gas fee (~$0.50-2.00)

**Listing Information**
- Ticket automatically appears in marketplace
- Buyers can see all event details
- Your wallet address is visible
- Listing timestamp recorded

### Step 4: Manage Your Sale

**While Listed**
- Monitor marketplace activity
- Track views and interest
- Adjust price if needed
- Respond to buyer questions

**When Sold**
- Automatic transfer to buyer
- Payment sent to your wallet
- Listing removed from marketplace
- Transaction recorded on blockchain

## 4. Pricing Strategies

### Market Analysis

**Research Current Prices**
1. Check all listings for same event
2. Note price ranges and trends
3. Identify average market price
4. Consider seat location differences

**Price History**
- Look at completed sales
- Track price changes over time
- Identify patterns and trends
- Learn from successful sellers

### Pricing Strategies

**Quick Sale Strategy**
- Price below market average
- Good for urgent sales
- Attracts buyers quickly
- Lower profit but guaranteed sale

**Market Rate Strategy**
- Price at current market average
- Balanced approach
- Reasonable profit margin
- Good for most situations

**Premium Strategy**
- Price above market (within limits)
- For high-demand events
- Premium seats or locations
- Requires patience

**Dynamic Pricing**
- Start high, reduce over time
- Adjust based on market activity
- Respond to competition
- Maximize profit potential

### Timing Considerations

**Early Listing (Weeks Before)**
- Higher prices possible
- More time to find buyers
- Less urgency from buyers
- Can adjust strategy

**Late Listing (Days Before)**
- Buyers more urgent
- Prices may be lower
- Quick sales more likely
- Less time for adjustments

## 5. Safety and Security

### Avoiding Scams

**Red Flags for Buyers**
- Prices significantly below market
- Sellers with no transaction history
- Requests for off-platform payment
- Pressure to buy immediately
- Suspicious wallet addresses

**Red Flags for Sellers**
- Buyers asking for discounts
- Requests to cancel and relist
- Unusual payment methods
- Pressure to accept low offers
- Communication outside platform

### Best Practices

**For Buyers**
- Verify ticket authenticity on blockchain
- Check seller's transaction history
- Compare prices across listings
- Use only the official marketplace
- Never send payment outside platform

**For Sellers**
- Price fairly within market range
- Respond promptly to inquiries
- Keep listings accurate and updated
- Use clear, honest descriptions
- Be patient with legitimate buyers

### Platform Protections

**Automatic Escrow**
- Payments held until transfer complete
- Prevents fraud and chargebacks
- Automatic release upon confirmation
- Dispute resolution available

**Blockchain Verification**
- All tickets verified on-chain
- Impossible to create fakes
- Transaction history transparent
- Ownership clearly established

## 7. Understanding Fees

### Fee Structure

**Platform Fee: 2.5%**
- Charged on sale price
- Covers platform operations
- Much lower than traditional platforms
- Transparent and predictable

**Organizer Royalty: 0-10%**
- Set by event organizer
- Supports artists and creators
- Varies by event type
- Clearly displayed before purchase

**Gas Fees**
- Network transaction costs
- Typically $0.25-2.00 on Base
- Varies with network congestion
- Paid by transaction initiator

### Fee Examples

**Example Sale: 0.12 ETH**
```
Sale Price: 0.12 ETH
Platform Fee (2.5%): 0.003 ETH
Organizer Royalty (5%): 0.006 ETH
Gas Fee: 0.001 ETH

Seller Receives: 0.11 ETH
Total Fees: 0.01 ETH (8.3%)
```

**Comparison to Traditional Platforms**
```
NFT Marketplace: 7.5-12.5% total fees
StubHub: 10-15% + payment processing
Ticketmaster Resale: 10-25%
Vivid Seats: 10-20%
```

### Fee Optimization

**For Sellers**
- Price to account for fees
- Consider total net proceeds
- Factor in gas costs
- Time listings strategically

**For Buyers**
- Compare total costs including fees
- Factor in gas for purchase
- Consider value vs. original price
- Budget for all expenses

## ðŸš¨ Common Issues & Solutions

### Listing Problems
**Issue**: Can't list ticket for sale
**Solutions**:
- Verify you own the ticket
- Check if transfers are allowed
- Ensure ticket isn't already used
- Confirm wallet connection

### Price Restrictions
**Issue**: Can't set desired price
**Solutions**:
- Check organizer's maximum price
- Verify current market rates
- Consider pricing within limits
- Contact support if unclear

### Transaction Failures
**Issue**: Purchase or sale fails
**Solutions**:
- Check wallet balance for gas
- Verify network connection
- Try again with higher gas
- Contact platform support

### Buyer/Seller Disputes
**Issue**: Problems with transaction
**Solutions**:
- Check blockchain transaction status
- Verify all details are correct
- Contact platform support
- Use dispute resolution process
